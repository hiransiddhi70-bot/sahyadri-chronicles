Culture & Traditions

🎉 Major Festivals of Maharashtra

🪔 Ganesh Chaturthi

📜 History: Public celebration of Ganesh Chaturthi was started by Lokmanya Bal Gangadhar Tilak in 1893 to unite people during India's freedom movement.

🙏 Importance: Dedicated to Lord Ganesha, the remover of obstacles and the God of wisdom, knowledge and prosperity.

🎊 Celebration: Beautiful idols are installed in homes and public pandals. Daily aarti, devotional songs, cultural programs and finally Ganesh Visarjan are performed after 10 days.

🍛 Traditional Food: Modak, Puran Poli and Karanji.

🎉 Gudhi Padwa

📜 History: Gudhi Padwa marks the beginning of the Hindu New Year and celebrates Lord Rama's victory over Ravana and Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj's victories.

🙏 Importance: It symbolizes success, prosperity, happiness and a fresh beginning.

🎊 Celebration: Families raise a Gudhi outside their homes, prepare rangoli, wear traditional clothes and enjoy festive meals.

🍛 Traditional Food: Shrikhand-Puri, Puran Poli and Neem-Jaggery mixture.

🪔 Diwali

📜 History: Diwali celebrates Lord Rama's return to Ayodhya after defeating Ravana and also honors Goddess Lakshmi.

🙏 Importance: Represents the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

🎊 Celebration: Homes are decorated with diyas, rangoli, lights, fireworks and families exchange sweets and gifts.

🍛 Traditional Food: Chakli, Ladoo, Karanji, Chivda and Shankarpali.

🌾 Makar Sankranti

📜 History: Celebrated when the Sun enters the zodiac sign Capricorn (Makara), marking the harvest season.

🙏 Importance: Symbolizes prosperity, gratitude and the beginning of longer days.

🎊 Celebration: Kite flying, family gatherings and exchanging Tilgul sweets with the message "Tilgul Ghya, God God Bola".

🍛 Traditional Food: Tilgul Ladoo, Gajak and Sesame sweets.

💃 Navratri

📜 History: Navratri is celebrated in honor of Goddess Durga and her victory over the demon Mahishasura.

🙏 Importance: Symbolizes courage, devotion, strength and the triumph of good over evil.

🎊 Celebration: Nine nights of fasting, prayers, Garba, Dandiya and cultural programs followed by Vijayadashami (Dussehra).

🍛 Traditional Food: Sabudana Khichdi, Kheer, Fruits and Fasting dishes.
